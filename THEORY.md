## Theoretical Premise

Institutional drift is not random deterioration. The pattern detected by this framework — progressive closure of correction pathways, decoupling of accountability, sustained constraint under rising harm — is structurally consistent with a specific underlying mechanism: the concentration of benefit in a subset of participants who have sufficient power to suppress the corrections that would interrupt it.

Option-space does not close by accident. Doors get locked when someone benefits from them being locked. Corrections get blocked when the correction would redistribute value away from whoever currently holds it. Accountability decouples when accountability becomes a direct threat to that position.

What varies across cases is not the mechanism but the architecture through which concentrated power persists — regulatory capture, operational opacity, executive insulation, coercive hierarchy — and the severity of collapse reflects how many structural layers existed between the benefiting group and consequence.

The variables in this framework do not measure intent. They measure the observable residue of that dynamic, expressed as system behavior over time.

---

## Cross-Domain Consistency

The same detection signature fires across three structurally different institutions because the underlying mechanism is invariant. Only the architecture of power concentration differs:

- **Boeing**: Engineering corrections to MAX design flaws were repeatedly overridden to protect certification timelines and production schedules. Power persisted through regulatory capture and executive override of internal safety processes.

- **UnitedHealthcare**: Denial appeal pathways and policy correction mechanisms were structurally constrained to sustain denial-rate margins. Power persisted through claims processing opacity and legal insulation from consequence.

- **Wells Fargo**: Sales pressure corrections were blocked to maintain aggressive cross-selling targets tied to executive compensation. Power persisted through coercive hierarchy that insulated senior leadership from accountability for frontline conduct.

This is why the same code, with no case-specific parameter changes, triggers a Forced Agency Collapse alert on all three datasets. The mechanism is the same. The residue it leaves in the variables is the same. The architecture is what differs.

## Why Oₜ Is the Control Variable

Oₜ (option-space) is decremented only by two event types: `option_eliminated` and `correction_blocked`. These flags are assigned strictly when primary sources explicitly document the structural removal or suppression of a correction pathway — no inference from hindsight, no interpretive reading of intent.

Each decrement is evidence of someone or some group successfully defending their position against redistribution or consequence. Oₜ is not a proxy score for general institutional health. It is a direct tracer of how many correction pathways remain structurally available. When it approaches zero while pressure remains high and harm continues rising, the system has been re-engineered — whether deliberately or incrementally — to protect an extractive equilibrium against the forces that would correct it.

---

## Falsifiability

This mechanism generates a testable prediction:

**High-pressure environments without concentrated benefit extraction — those with distributed accountability or strong external correction checks — should show elevated Cₜ but stable Oₜ and no FAC trigger.**

If an institution is under genuine external pressure but its internal correction pathways remain open and accountability remains coupled to outcomes, the framework should not fire. A control case demonstrating this would confirm that FAC detection is not simply a measure of institutional stress, but of structural resistance to correction under stress.

Interrater reliability testing and control case validation are the identified next methodological steps before formal publication.

---

## Implications for AI Governance

The same mechanism applies to any system where optimization pressure benefits a narrow objective at the expense of broader correction capacity. An AI agent operating under sustained reward pressure, with internal correction pathways being suppressed or bypassed, and with oversight accountability degrading over time, would produce the same variable signature.

If an agent's internal event logs show Oₜ collapsing — correction attempts blocked, goal adjustment pathways closed — while constraint load spikes and harm metrics rise, that is the same pattern. The appropriate response is escalation to human oversight before overt failure occurs, not after.

The framework does not require intent. It requires observable residue. That makes it applicable wherever structured event logs exist.
